# indigenous_target_sports_catalogue2 info session # Indigenous Target Sports Initiative > **Building Niger's next generation of competitive traditional sports** A strategic framework for cultural preservation, youth empowerment, creative enterprise, and international sporting excellence — rooted in African heritage. --- ## What this is This interactive catalogue presents a comprehensive vision for transforming indigenous target sports across Niger and the Sahel into nationally recognized, internationally competitive disciplines. Built on the proven success of Niger's **Sabre National** traditional wrestling model, the initiative maps a clear path from village play to the World Indigenous Games. ## Live catalogue **[View the interactive presentation →](https://ADAI-nethub.github.io/indigenous_target_sports_catalogue2.html/)** The catalogue covers seven strategic sections: | Section | What's inside | |---|---| | **Why indigenous target sports?** | Strategic rationale: cultural preservation, youth engagement, sport for development, tourism, creative industries, economic opportunity, cultural diplomacy | | **The games** | Detailed profiles of Ishighän, Dilli, Jukskei, Mngqwayi, San stick throwing, and Maracana — origins, skills, competition potential, and STEM learning value | | **Niger's winning blueprint** | The Sabre National ecosystem model: Government → Federation → Leagues → Championship → Sponsors → Media → International recognition | | **Partnerships** | 10 stakeholder organizations across policy, governance, Olympic pathway, corporate, research, heritage, tourism, and community development | | **Competition pyramid** | 7-level pathway from Village Clubs to World Indigenous Games | | **Economic opportunities** | 12 livelihood categories and 4 sector deep-dives | | **Roadmap 2027–2032** | 7-phase implementation plan from research to Pan-African recognition | ## Target audience - **Policymakers** — Ministry of Youth, Sports & Culture - **Sports federations** — FENILUTTE, CNOSN, ANOCA - **Corporate sponsors** — Airtel Niger, Niger Télécoms - **Development organizations** — NGOs, UN agencies, bilateral partners - **Academic institutions** — Universities, museums, research centres - **Tourism agencies** — Destination development and cultural route planners ## The vision > "By building on Niger's successful traditional wrestling model, Indigenous Target Sports can become a new platform for cultural preservation, youth empowerment, creative enterprise, and international sporting excellence. Together, government, federations, businesses, and communities can transform ancestral games into a globally recognized movement rooted in African innovation." ## LinkedIn campaign This catalogue supports a 3-part LinkedIn series exploring how Niger could become Africa's innovation hub for Indigenous Target Sports. **Hashtags:** #Niger #TraditionalGames #SportInnovation #CreativeEconomy #AfricanHeritage #YouthDevelopment #Culture #TargetSports #Sahel #Innovation --- ## About the initiative The Indigenous Target Sports Initiative is a call to action for coordinated investment in traditional sport ecosystems across the Sahel. It demonstrates that the future of sport may already exist within our traditions — waiting only for structure, investment, and vision to unlock its potential. **Pillars:** Culture • Competition • Community • Creative Economy --- *Strategic Framework | 2027–2032*
